  • PHONE BILL-BOARD

    Design Exercise for redesigning a phone bill inspired by a dashboard design.

    Role

    UX/UI Design, Interactive Prototyping

    Problem

    I have used T-mobile as my carrier for two years. Whenever I logged in and tried to find what was a problem of this month's bill bomb, I often feel frustrated. Phonebill is worthy to redesign. For analysing all pain points, I looked over all process end-to-end and expressed it into a user-flow diagram.

    Pain Points

    • Users should find a target link -> click -> load a new page (even it takes some minutes). Too many pages!
    • Summary and details about same category of information are dispalyed in a different pages.
    • Need to be more visual. Text-based information with a lack of filters.
    • Needs a quick tool to move on the timeline for comparison.

    Questions

    What if

    users get a summary of information and related details at once?

    How to

    avoid to load new pages and access data with consistency?

    What is

    the efficient way to display information with clear navigation?

    Solution

    Informational Navigation

    A Navigation includes the visualized thumbnail information (e.g. Amount Owed / Due dates / Total usages.) Also, in a narrow scope of data visualization, a part of diagrams can be a navigation to get the more detailed visualization.

    Access all data in less clicks

    Users can get all information in few clicks without loading a new page. Dynamic contents with a cross-filtering can be a solution.
